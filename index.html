About

It’s well known that feedback is critical when evaluating something or when gathering data for a market analysis. Nevertheless, it is an expensive and sometimes slow process that may not be as precise as we need it to be.

W. Chan Kim and Renée Mauborgne, professors at INSEAD and co-directors of the INSEAD Blue Ocean Strategy Institute published a book in 2005: Blue Ocean Strategy. In this book, they unveil this marketing theory. They represent very competitive industries as red oceans. These reference companies that struggle to innovate and target specific markets while competing against lots of other companies, doing the same thing, with the same kind of product. In the other hand, a blue ocean represents an unexplored industry, an opportunity of actual innovation, and a market nobody else has identified before.

Navy Chart is tailored to help those unidentified markets reveal themselves and for stakeholders to have them in sight. As it name suggests, the application will act as a chart that could lead any organization, developer, or institution, out of those red waters and into new navy-blue oceans of opportunities. This application will help not only big companies, but any stakeholder who has the power to target those blue ocean markets with new innovative products.

Features

local_offer

Tag based classification

Blue markets are classified and divided with tags. These help users identify the industry involved in an opportunity or problem.

search

Blue market search

Users can search for blue markets by popularity, date, and associated tags. They can bookmark their favorites to keep track of them later.

brush

Paint new markets

Paint an existing market to turn it into a darker blue. Navy blue markets indicate a greater market impact.
